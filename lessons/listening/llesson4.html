Lesson 4: Note-taking Basics

Learn how to listen smart and write faster than your thoughts.

Why Note-taking Matters

In IELTS Listening, writing down everything isn’t the goal but catching the right things is. Smart note-taking helps you stay focused, remember details, and catch keywords before they slip away.

1. Write Less, Understand More

  • Don’t copy full sentences, but rather focus on main ideas.
  • Use arrows (->), short forms, and abbreviations like “info” or “b4”.
  • Keep your notes clear and structured, not messy scribbles.

Tip: Note only what helps you answer, not what sounds “nice.” Be selective.

2. Use Visual Clues

Use symbols, brackets, or small sketches to organize ideas quickly.

  • “⬆” for increase, “⬇” for decrease
  • “💲” for money or prices
  • “📍” for location or place

Tip: Your notes should speak to you, not your teacher. Personalize your symbols.
