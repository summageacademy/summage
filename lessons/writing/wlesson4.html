Lesson 4: Formal Letter Structure

How to Write a Polished and Professional Letter

Why Structure Matters

In Writing Task 1 for formal letters, clear structure is essential. It shows examiners that you can communicate politely and professionally.

1 Greeting and Opening

  • Dear Sir or Madam
  • Dear Mr Smith / Ms Jones
  • Start with a polite introduction explaining why you are writing

Tip: Always match your greeting with the formality of the situation.

2 Body of the Letter

  • Use one paragraph for each main point
  • Be clear and concise
  • Use polite and professional phrases such as "I would be grateful if" or "I am writing to request"
  • Include details or examples to support your points

Tip: Keep your tone formal but friendly, avoid slang or overly casual words.

3 Closing and Sign Off

  • Use phrases like "Yours faithfully" if you don't know the name
  • "Yours sincerely" if you know the name
  • End with a polite note or request

Tip: Your closing leaves a lasting impression, make it polite and professional.
