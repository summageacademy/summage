YouTube Channels for Improving Listening

List of Best YouTube Channels to Improve English Listening

If you are an english learner and want to improve your listening comprehension and speaking skills, watching english content and surronding yourself with english environment is the bast way to do so.

YouTube is the best platform is you want to achieve your desired goals in language learning. It is free, you can find variety of videos and most improtantly they are real world speeches.

We have made a list of best YouTube channels so that you don't have to spend time researching on your own.

For Beginner Learners

If you are a beginner level student, you mostly need to watch videos with slow speech and simple vocabulary, so that you can build momentum and actually understand the meaning of videos. You might also want to turn on subtitles. Trust me, they help a lot at the beginning.

You must start with channels like BBC Learning English and VOA Learning English. They offer what most beginner learners need, which is simple speech and useful vocabulary.

As you keep watching such videos, they might start to feel little easy for you. That's when you know you have to move to the next level.

For Intermediate Learners

If you are an intermediate student, then MrBeast and other lifestyle bloggers will be the best choice for now.

MrBeast's videos have clear speech and the content itself is very engaging. If you are bored with dull beginner videos, his videos fit the best for your desired content.

You can also watch lifestyle or traveling vloggers alongside. Channels like Kara and Nate, Rick Steves can be the best fit. They speak in clear American accent, their videos are usually in storytelling style and they mostly use daily-life english.

Additionally, Learn English With TV Series channel is also helpful as they can be the first door to watching podcasts. This channel breaks down podcasts to simple english content.

For Advanced Learners

For those who are Advanced students, watching TED talks and Interviews with celebrities can be really helpful. You will get used to comples ideas, faster speech and different accents. These benefits are very useful in your daily life too, because let's be honest, native speakers do not talk to you like a beginner.

You can also watch motivational videos but some students might find them a little bit boring. Try watching them and see if they fit your taste. Channels like Motivation Ark and MotivationHub offer such videos and they get really good reviews, too.

There are also podcasts and TV shows on YouTube like Jay Shetty Podcast. Podcasts are very useful to start thinking about complex ideas in english, too.

How to actually learn

This is the most important part. For the beginning, you will have to watch with subtitles. You have to let your brain get used to english content first.

And After watching with subtitles, rewatch the same video with subtitles turned off. This helps to smoothe the transition from watching with subtitles to watching any content entirely without subtitles at all.

Also, you can pause throughout the video and repeat sentences. This way you are improving both you listening and speaking skills simultaneously. This can be called shadowing technique, too.

You might also want to write down new vocabulary you learn from content you watch. Even if you write down 2 or 3 words per video, the numbers can compound overtime.

Common Mistakes Students Do

These are the most important mistakes students must avoid

Beginner level students must not start watching content that is too hard for the first time. Beginner might struggle to understand what is going on in the video and the efficency might drop.

Binge-watching without proper focus is also a very common mistake lots of students do. It is better to watch one video fully focused rather than watching 10 videos in a row with poor concentration.

Students must not rely on subtitles forever. Let's be honest here, subtitles are not always available and you might become too dependent on them and might even struggle to improve your listening skills.
