Guide on How to Imporve Your Listening Score

Many Students struggle to achieve their desired score in IELTS Listening. This is mostly caused by lack of focus or concentration or simply student did not practice enough before exam...

In this blog post, we will explore listening practice strategies and how can a student be consistent in preparation.

English Content

One of the best ways to practice and improve listening skills is simply by watching english content. It can be your favourite movies, listening to english music with lyrics or watching your favourite vlog YouTuber.

These contents will help you build english environment around yourself. A student who is from non-english speaking country likely will not have full english environment. Therefore, that student will struggle to comprehend what he listens and might even struggle to speak!

As learning new language is generally same as a baby learning new words from his parents. He listens and after comprehending what it means, he speaks. He is simulateously improving his speaking and listening skills.

Let's get deeper because beginner can not just start watching advanced content. So where should you start and how to gradually jump into watching "advanced" content.

It is recommended for beginners to start watching kids cartoons, slow english learning videos or short educational videos. Good examples are BBC Learning English, VOA learning English or cartoons like Bluey.

As you gradually improve your listeing with such contents, you can look into sitcoms, lifestyle bloggers, travel vlogs and beginner podcasts.

Adcanced students' main goal must be handling faster speech and different accents. Therefore, documentaries, interviews and TED talks would be amazing for them.

You can also watch IELTS speaking videos online. I'm telling you, these are very useful for english learners.

By that being said, do not forget to watch our videos on our YouTube Channel too.

Practice with listening materials.

Practicing with english audio tests is as important as other ways of improving listeing skills. It is essential, especially for students who are preparing for exam rather than for those who are just trying to improve their listeing comprehension.

To apply this preparation method into your daily routine, you can look for online listening tests. Online Platforms like Summage Academy offers such tools, where students can practice listening tests for completely free.

However, if you have a printer or just prefer offline listening test practice, you can look into our PDF test files. Also, you can buy decent listening tests book for more practice.

You might also want to try listening to podcasts. They can be useful to let your ears get used to english conversations.

Stop Translating English in Your Head

Many students translate english and try to understand what the speaker is saying. That is what stops you from getting into next level of listening. You must not try to "translate" it, but rather, you must practice thinking in english so that you do not need time to translate.

To start thinking in english, as we earlier mentioned, you need english environment. No matter how much practice tests you take, you can not stop translating unless you use or hear english daily. English must become part of your daily life.

One more "hack" to create english life around yourself is to follow english social media content makers. If social media is big part of your daily life, this must help turn your feed into your desired environment. The more english content you consume, the more it helps to understand what you hear.

You can also try reading english books. Books will force your brain to start thinking in english. Unlike videos where visuals are already ready, Books, on the other hand, will give reader time to visualize what is going on in the page.

The method below can help you fix "translating" issue efficiently too.

Talking with English Speakers

Oh, There is no better preparation method for both speaking and listening, than having conversations with native or english speakers. This method is so useful, that you would improve your skills significantly in a small amount of time without much effort.

Now, you might have a question, where can you even find people who would love to talk? Here is the answer, simply look for local clubs near you. This might sound too basic recomendation and might feel like hard task for people who do not live in english country.

But here is the catch. No matter where you are from, there are always youth-led clubs or events, taking place in Youth Creativity Centers. Or simply find someone who is also learning english and practice together.

And if there is truly no one to talk with, then you can try online platforms like Ibrat Academy. Ibrat Academy can connect you with other english learners and you can practice together.

If you want to make native friends rather than just anonymous talk for 10 minutes with strangers, you might want to try apps like HelloTalk and Tandem. There, you can chat and make new connections with those who are learning new languages.

Turn off the Subtitles

Let's be honest. Subtitles are indeed useful when you are new to english. But over time, brain might get used to them and struggle when they are off.

Social media might have made our brains to always look for subtitles. Nowadays, subtitles can be found almost in every video.

By that being said, if you are already intermediate level student, do not watch movies or videos with subtitles turned on next time, if you want to take your listeing to next level.
