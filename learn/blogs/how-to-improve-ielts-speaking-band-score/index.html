Guide on How to Improve Your Speaking Score

You can read and think in english. You understand what you hear in english. But when it comes to speaking, you freeze as if every english word you know just disappeared at once.

Especially in the exam, you do not know what to say or you make obvious mistakes while speaking under pressure.

If this is exactly your problem, we will fix it in this blog. Make sure to take notes and actually start practicing afterwards.

How IELTS Speaking is scored

Most of the readers here must be searching solution for IELTS speaking related issues. The examiner gives you a band score from 0-9 based on 4 speaking criteria and here is info about them.

There are 4 criteria in IELTS Speaking. Fluency & Coherance, Lexical resources which is basically Vocabulary, Grammatical Range & Accuracy and Pronunciation.

Fluency & Coherance: How smoothly you speak and how well your ideas connect.

Lexical Resources: Range of words or phrases you use and ability to express ideas clearly.

Grammatical Range & Accuracy: How correct your grammar is and how complex your sentences are.

Pronunciation: How easy you are to understand and how well you speak words.

In simple words, to get high score in IELTS Speaking, you need to speak clearly and confidently rather than trying to speak "perfectly".

Why Students Struggle?

These below are the main causes that stop you from speaking confidently in english.

One of the most common issues is that students think too much about grammar before or while speaking. This leads to awkward stops and "repeating" in between speech. Students constantly think of grammar and try to be grammatically perfect and if they recognize they made a mistake, they will repeat the sentence to correct the mistake.

Another issue is translating from your own native language to english in your head. This also leads to that awkward silence in between speech.

Lack of Speaking Practice is also one of the biggest factors among the causes. If you do not practice beforehand, how do you expect speaking to go well in real situations?

Most important part, students get really nervous and fear that people might judge them, even though lots of people want to help if you were to make mistake.

Below are the best ways to fix your problems with speaking.

Speak Every Day

As I mentioned previously, practice is the key to success. You must practice english everyday and make the language part of your daily life. This is the most essential step you can do towards speaking success.

You can start simple just by describing your day to the mirror or just talking to yourself throughout the day. Make sure to record yourself and see where you are making mistakes. Once you stop your mistakes, avoid making them next time.

Stop Translating in Your Head

Translating in your head is the best way to lower your speaking score, honestly speaking. Do not even think of translating while you are speaking! There is a blog of ours you can read to learn more about it: How to Start Thinking in English

Use simple words

You better not try to use complex words in your speech. Band 7 in IELTS does not require you to use most complex, extraordinary words in the known dictionary. Rather, you must speak clearly and confidently. Clarity in your speech is much better than any big fancy word.

Practice with real questions

If you are preparing for IELTS, we recommend you to prepare with real questions. They help simulate real exam conditions and actually get your brain used to such questions.

Shadowing Technique

You must also improve your pronunciation. Clarity matter much more than sounding British or American. You must say the words clearly so the other person in the conversation understand you easily and this is very useful both in exam situations and real life.

Today, watch your favourite YouTuber or Vlogger and try to repeat the phrases and words after them. This is what we call shadowing and it helps you get that natural speech.

Learn Useful Phrases

It is better for you to memorize usefull phrases and collocations rather than memorizing answers for each question. Examiners can detect if a student has memorized answers or speaking naturally. Memorized answers can affect your speaking score negatively.

Sections of IELTS Speaking

For Part 1, Keep the answers short and try to sound more natural. Do not add too much detail to your answers but dont sound dull too.

For Part 2, you must speak around 2 minutes. You must have a decent structure in your speech, starting with intro, and then details and ending with an example.

Part 3 is more like logical test. You must answer with logic and most of the time, part 3 questions will be linked to Part 2. Feel free to give your opinions and explain ideas here.

Common Mistakes

Do not memorize answers, Do not pause for too long, Do not use too much complex grammar and most improtantly be confident and don't speak too fast as it makes you look nervous.
