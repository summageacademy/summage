How to Stop Translating in Your Head

Yes, you undestrand english but too slowly... Today is the time to fix it!

You translate every sentence or word you hear into your native language in your head and it feels like native speakers speak too fast. Or you might even find speaking challenging because you need extra time to translate the speech in your brain first.

If this is exactly your problem, then below are the techniques and ways to fix this issue.

The Root Cause

Think About It. Why do you even translate at the first place? If you are unsure about the answer, then I will explain simply. Your Brain is used to your native language and you lack the ability to recall vocabulary automatically. You might also be trying to speak grammatically perfect, too.

These issues are exactly what holding you back and you need to fix them as soon as possible.

Translating is slowing you down

Imagine you are in the middle of a conversation. It is your turn to speak but first, you have to translate things you want to say in your head before you speak. It leads to that awkward silence and delay between talking.

Translating in your head also breaks listening flow. You will get stuck trying to translate the first sentence you hear while speaker is already on the third sentence. On top of that, it is impossible to keep up with fast speech with translating.

How to fix this issue?

The best way to fix this issue for new learners is by simply stop chasing grammatically perfect translations in your head. Most of the time, you only need to use fast and simple english in your head to process what is going on or what you are going to speak.

You must think about core idea of what you are going to speak or what you are listening. If someone is telling you to go to supermarket and buyr groceries, you do not translate it word by word to your native language, right?

You must think of main objects and main ideas. Take the example we gave you bit earlier. You must think in very basic and plain english. "Go to supermarket", "Buy groceries".

After reading this blog, try thinking in simple english. Keep all the sentences in your head basic and plain.

Use Imagination

Let's say you hear the word "car". Do not search for word in your native language at all! Instead of words, use images of the objects. Imagine a car in your head and simply name it in english. That is basically how you build momentum and start thinking in english.

How long does it take?

Honestly, don't expect to start thinking fully in english within a week of practice. Weeks of practice delivers only basic improvements. But with consistent practice throughout months will give you ability to naturally think in english.

3 months of slow progress is much better than 1 day spike of motivation and letting go the flow.

Common Mistakes Learners Do

Most students try to be too perfect in the very beginning. But how do you learn from your mistakes and actually imrpove if you consistently avoid making mistakes.

Do not fear making mistakes but rather fear being stuck because you avoid them.
