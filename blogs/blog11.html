TOEFL or IELTS? What Are the Differences Between Them?

A clear and friendly guide to understanding which exam suits you best based on your goals, abilities and study style.

Choosing between TOEFL and IELTS can feel confusing. Both tests measure your English level, but they are designed differently and each one fits different types of learners.

Here is a simple explanation that will help you understand how both exams work and which one will be more comfortable for you.

TOEFL focuses mostly on academic English. The tasks feel like real university situations such as listening to lectures, reading long passages and writing structured essays. It is usually taken on a computer.

IELTS is more flexible and feels more like real life communication. The reading and listening sections contain shorter and more varied texts. The speaking part is a face to face interview with an examiner.

TOEFL speaking is done on a microphone. You speak to the computer and your voice is recorded. Some students find this easier because they do not feel shy. Others prefer a human examiner, which makes IELTS a better choice for them.

TOEFL writing requires two essays. One is based on reading and listening. The other is an independent opinion essay. IELTS writing includes describing a graph or diagram and writing an opinion or argument essay.

IELTS offers both paper based and computer based versions. This makes it comfortable for students who prefer writing by hand or typing depending on their style.

TOEFL uses only one scoring style. IELTS gives a band score for each skill. This means you can see your strengths more clearly if you choose IELTS.

Both exams are accepted by thousands of universities. However, some countries prefer one over the other. For example, many American universities are more familiar with TOEFL, while IELTS is often preferred in the UK, Europe and Australia.

Take time to reflect on your strengths. If you are comfortable with computers, academic style texts and structured tasks, go for TOEFL. If you prefer human interaction, flexible questions and a more real life style exam, choose IELTS.

Small summary

Key differences between TOEFL and IELTS

  • TOEFL is computer based and feels more academic while IELTS feels more real life
  • TOEFL speaking is recorded on a microphone while IELTS speaking is a conversation with an examiner
  • IELTS offers paper based and computer based options while TOEFL is mainly computer based
  • Both exams are accepted worldwide so choose the one that matches your strengths and comfort
